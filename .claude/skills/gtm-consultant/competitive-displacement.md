# Competitive Displacement Playbook

Over 60% of B2B software purchases are replacements, not greenfield. This is the majority of the addressable market.

## Step 1: Identify Accounts Using Competitors

**Data sources for tech stack identification:**
- ZoomInfo, BuiltWith, HG Insights for technographic data
- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius for review mining and intent
- Job postings mentioning competitor tools (e.g., "Salesforce Admin" = they use Salesforce)
- LinkedIn job descriptions revealing tech requirements
- Website source code analysis (tracking pixels, scripts, integrations)

**nrev-lite approach:**
- `nrev_google_search(query="[competitor] site:g2.com/products", tbs="year")`
- `nrev_search_patterns(use_case="competitive_intelligence")` for platform-specific patterns
- `nrev_enrich_company` for technographic data

## Step 2: Identify Dissatisfaction Signals

**High-intent signals:**
- Searches for "[Competitor] alternatives" or "cancel [Competitor]"
- Negative reviews on G2/Capterra (especially recent)
- Support complaints on social media/forums
- Competitor pricing changes or feature removals (mass dissatisfaction)
- Competitor acquisition or leadership turnover (uncertainty)

**Organizational signals:**
- New executive hires (new leaders bring new tool preferences)
- Reorganizations or strategic pivots
- Budget cycle timing (Q4 planning for Q1 spend)

## Step 3: Time Your Outreach

**Contract renewal cycles:**
- Target ~90 days before contract expiration
- Track adoption dates to predict renewal timing
- Enterprise auto-renewal cancellation windows: 30-60 days
- Multi-year contracts at $50K+ ACV = tighter windows

## Step 4: Displacement Messaging

**Three value axes that matter to switching buyers:**
1. **Time to value** — how fast productive on your platform?
2. **Total cost to switch** — migration effort, training, productivity dip, risk
3. **Perceived risk** — what if new solution is worse?

**Messaging principles:**
- Address switching risk directly and honestly
- Lead with migration help and onboarding support
- Use case studies from customers who switched from named competitors
- "Switch-and-save" offers, contract buyouts, free migration
- Never directly attack the competitor
- Use competitor review feedback as messaging fuel

**Target the full buying committee (5-7+ stakeholders):**
- End user: wants features
- Manager: wants efficiency
- Executive: wants ROI
- Procurement: wants cost savings

## Step 5: Measure

Teams using technographic targeting see:
- 28% higher conversion rates
- 50% more likely to exceed revenue goals
- 27% shorter sales cycles
- Top performers: 75% competitive displacement success rates

One team achieved 54% more scheduled meetings by incorporating competitor review insights into outreach.
